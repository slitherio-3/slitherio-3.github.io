    The beginning when the car was a very new invention, a car race was all about proving that a vehicle is quick, practical and safe to use. It was not so much a question of covering the distance to the destination, but above all of proving that you were able to get to your destination with your car. The first cars actually looked more like motorized carriages than the modern vehicles we see today.

    Later, after driving became more and more a reliable form of transportation, the focus of car racing shifted to manufacturers who could prove the endurance and power of their engines. In the 1930s, the brands began to develop cars specifically for car racing.
